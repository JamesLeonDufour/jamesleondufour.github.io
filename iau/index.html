PHILIPPINES

OCHA helps the world’s most vulnerable people by coordinating global humanitarian response. It does this in five ways: coordination, advocacy, information management, funding and writing policy.

In January 2014, OCHA staff were coordinating relief assistance in the Philippines where 14 million people were suffering from the devastating effects of  Super Typhoon Haiyan.

SOUTH SUDAN

In February, South Sudan was classified as a Level 3 emergency  ̶  he worst level of humanitarian crisis. Over 800,000 people had fled their homes.

With the country confronting potential famine,  OCHA worked with the humanitarian community to develop an appeal in mid-2014 to help four million South Sudanese in dire need of humanitarian aid.
